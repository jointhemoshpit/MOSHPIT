Brand Vision

At MOSHPIT, we're not just about the music—we're about creating connections so strong they might upend the planet. We picture a badass community where rock enthusiasts from around the world assemble to erupt on stage with enthusiasm and passion. We are the beating heart of a movement, where every headbang is a tribute to the strength of solidarity. We are not just fans. The stage is our temple, and the riff is our language in this thrilling sanctuary. Raise your horns, seize the lightning, and come party with us in the MOSHPIT, where the rock revolution is raging!

Brand Name

We went deep into the heart of the rock community in our attempt to establish a platform that reflects the actual spirit of rock culture and encourages real connections among fans. One word kept coming up in our problem interviews and while doing in-depth research: "moshpit." For many outsiders, the moshpit may evoke images of chaos, violence, and danger—misconceptions often associated with heavy metal and rock music. But in the music scene, the moshpit is considered something much deeper. It is a place of purity, a haven for individual expression, and a representation of unity. Though it may not seem like it, the moshpit is a safe sanctuary where people gather to let loose, experience the music in their veins, and create relationships that cut over barriers. The word "moshpit" kept coming up in our problem interviews, highlighting its importance in the rock world. It became clear that this term captures the essence of what we want to represent: passion, unity, and the raw spirit of rock 'n' roll. By selecting "MOSHPIT" as our brand name, we honor this lasting element of the rock genre. We celebrate its actual essence—a place where listeners can be authentic and feel a strong connection to the music—and accept its duality, eliminating myths about it.

    Visual Identity and Supporting Visuals

    • Colour Palette

      Following the Problem Interviews, which helped us define our brand better, we have created a color palette based on what colours the target audience thought represent the hard rock community the best in the interviews. Therefore, our colour palette is the following:

      • #202020 The colour black was the most mentioned in our Problem Interviews as a representative colour for rock music. However, we chose not to use pure black, but rather a shade of black that keeps the design bold but it’s easier for the eye and more readable.
      • #C3252F Red was the second most mentioned colour by our target audience and it represents energy, passion and excitement, which also matches with how people described the rock community and what we want to associate our brand with.
      • #272C7E Blue signifies trust and dependability, values that we want people to feel through our product, and we chose a shade of dark blue as it creates a bold design.
      • #FDD838 Yellow represents joy and promotes fun, which is also what we want to make people feel through our events. Moreover, it is another one of the mentioned colours by our target audience and we used it to create a bold contrast with the red and blue shades.
      • #717276: As we already had 3 bold colors, we chose to also have grey as another neutral color in order to create a balance to the overall visual composition of the website.

        • References for the color justification can be found in Appendix References.

    • Font choices

      • For our website, we chose only two fonts to keep the website consistent and cohesive: DeLittle Chromatic for the headlines, and Rockwell for other text.

      We have created the logo ourselves, for which we used sharp lines and edges for each letter in order to create a bold design. The font DeLittle Chromatic matched a lot with the style of our logo, which is why we chose it to keep the website cohesive with the brand’s identity. Moreover, the bold design of the font creates a strong impression and captures the reader’s attention.

      However, to create a balance and not overwhelm the user, we chose a simpler, easier for the eye font for the rest of the text. While not as sharp as DeLittle Chromatic, the Rockwell font has a subtle angularity by keeping a balance between sharp and rounded elements, adding a touch of dynamism to the text.

  • Logo

    • For our logo we created two versions: a main logo with the full name of the brand and a secondary logo that has a different layout in order to be adaptable to various formats.
    Our main logo was created by drawing each letter with sharp lines and edges in order to make them look bold and unique. Moreover, instead of aligning the letters in a straight line, we put them in a wavy pattern to represent the crowd movements at a concert, where people create a wave-like effect when raising their hands.
    For our second logo, in order to make it smaller and adaptable to formats like social media profiles, we decided to use only the M from our main logo and actually create an illustration of people's hands raised at a concert. We decided to use our two main colors (black and red) as they were the ones most associated with the rock commmunity in our problem interviews.

  • Supporting visuals
    • Our supporting visuals include pictures of crowds and venues, as our service is about rock events. We searched specifically for pictures that are bold and represent the good energy we want at our own events. However, we would use actual pictures from our own events once our first ones happen. Moreover, for the meet the team pictures found here we decided to use black and white in order to create a striking visual contrast and puts focus on each person, while also keeping the consistency of our brand's colors with colorful cards and text. We also chose to display a picture of the team together to be a visual representation of cooperation and trust, as it represents the values we have within our events and community. Moreover, it makes our brand more approachable and relatable as it makes it easier for people to connect with us when they see the faces behind it not only separately, but also united. For the team picture, we chose to give it red and blue tones rather than black and white as it makes the picture feel bold, fun and consistent with our color palette. Giving the team picture a different color from the individual pictures is also meant to empathize the individual value of each member.

    Testing Report

    Testing Round

    In order to see whether our website if fully functional and whether there are things that need to be improved, we are conducting a testing round with 4 participants within our target group. The participants are 3 females and 1 male, they are between 20 and 24 years old. Lastly one thing all participants have in common is that they are part of the hard rock community. These people are yet unknown to our product and will therefore have an uninformed position. To properly test the functions of our website we gave our participants the following tasks to do on within our website on a computer. We will record with our phone to be able to look back on the testing and be able to draw more conclusions.

      Tasks for the participants to do:
    • Go to the event calendar page
    • Go to the meet the team page
    • Go to the chats page
    • Go back to the homepage
    • Send a message in an event chat
    • Add a friend/ group chat
    • Find information about Vladislava
    • Find information about the MOSHPIT event in Utrecht and buy a ticket
    • Go the 4 brand values and read the full text
    • Find contact information about the brand and go to our Instagram page

    If any struggle or confusion is seen at the participant, the interviewee will ask what the problem is and what in their opinion could be a solution.

    By giving the participants these tasks, we can fully test the functions of our website and if they work the way they should and if our target audience think it is logical how the website works.

    When the participant finished the tasks, we will ask some follow up questions to get their opinion on the product and the features of it. The questions we will ask will be as followed.

      Questions:
    • What did you think of the functionality of the website?
    • Do you think it demonstrates the products, we as a brand offer well?
    • Do you think any improvements can be made?

    Video of the testing can be found in Appendix Production.

    Findings

      By observing the participant navigation through our website, we concluded the following problems with our website:
    • While searching for the brand values participants went to the about us dropdown menu and couldn’t find the values there. Afterwards they were confused where to find the values and even looked over them.
    • While searching for the brand contact information some participants went to the about us tab in order to find the contact information. After they saw it wasn’t there their first instinct was to go the bottom of the page and could find our contact information. However, it was not their first thought to search there.

    After giving the participants tasks, we asked them the follow-up questions. The results from the questions where as followed. The participants thought the website was highly functionable with smooth functions. The website clearly shows the products we offer as a brand well along with the information about it. Lastly, things are easy to find. However, participants didn’t like that that there is no symbol to click on so you can send a message. Besides they think there should be separate pages for finding our contact information and for the brand values. Lastly, they think that adding a paragraph with information about the brand MOSHPIT would be beneficial.

    To conclude the positive and negative findings of the testing round the following points can be found below in order of importance:

      Positive findings:
    • The products we offer as a brand are clear along with the information about our products
    • The website is highly functionable, and information is easy to find
    • The functions (buttons, navigation bars etc.) the website offers are very smooth
      Negative Findings:
    • The values and contact information of the brand are hard to find which makes users confused
    • On the chatroom page there is no button to click on in order to send your message
    • Information given about the brand MOSHPIT is seen as essential by participants

    Now that we have collected the findings, we can draw some conclusions on what we can improve on our website, in order to make it more user friendly.

    Improvement Suggestions

    From both our observations and follow up questions we can improve the following aspects of our website. A button to send the messages should be added to the chatroom page. Besides this we should add to the “About Us” dropdown menu two extra pages. One that is called “About MOSHPIT” and another called “Contact”. In the “About MOSHPIT” page we can give information about the brand like the values mission and vision and the requested paragraph about the brand MOSHPIT. In the “Contact” page we present all our contact information. This way if the users of our website are searching for information about the brand and our contact information, they should be faster in finding it, and there will be less confusion with this improved version.

      To sum this up the following things should be changed within the website to make it more user-friendly:
    • Within the dropdown menu “About Us” we need to add two extra pages that are “Contact” and “About MOSHPIT”
    • Add a send button to the chatroom page
    • Add a paragraph with information about the brand MOSHPIT to our website

    With these improvements we will continue to improve our website. After improving, we will do another round of testing to see whether there is more confusing situations or things to improve.

    Context of campaign and promotional activities

    Campaign

    The marketing campaign of MOSHPIT has the goal to brand awareness of a new product in a niche market. Furthermore, the aim is to generate community reach and engagement that we measure with the help of objectives that we set for the first four weeks of the campaign (can be found below). The message the campaign aimed to portray is: “Put your horns up and get ready to create meaningful experiences and rock-solid connection by joining the MOSHPIT”. The promotional activities executed for the campaign were chosen based on channel/platform choices and in accordance with the target audience preferences. It all circled back to original content creation. Original color palettes, logos and visuals were crafted and helped to make the brand resonate with the target audience and keep the consistency between posts. Following that, official posters, informational and entertainment videos/posts were made and shared through the social media chosen. This is also discussed below and can be found in the Communication and Media Plan made specifically for the campaign. The Communication and Media Plan can also be found in Appendix Marketing.

    Objectives

    The marketing objectives set for the campaign of MOSHPIT are as follows:

      Reach objective: By 1st of April 2024 the following objectives should be reached: 
    • Instagram: 200 account interactions from the different content posted (reels, posts, stories).   
    • TikTok: 100 accounts interactions of the content (TikToks) posted. 
      •  
      Affect objective: A sense of community for hard rock fans.
      Response objective: By 1st of April 2024 the following objectives should be reached: 
    • Instagram: 100 followers. Average daily engagement rate after 30 days is expected to be 2,5%. An engagement rate by post will be done on the last post posted for the month and expectations are to be 5%.  
    • TikTok: 50 followers. Average daily engagement rate after 30 days is expected to be 2,5%. An engagement rate by post will be done on the last post posted for the month and expectations are to be 5%. 
    • Website: 50 clicks should be generated. 

    Keeping in mind that we will only measure the results from 4 weeks, we decided not to be too ambitious and set small and easily achievable objectives that can give us an overview of how close we got to the set goal for the campaign. Furthermore, we used exact numbers and specific context in which they will be measured.

    Channels/Platforms

    The platforms chosen to carry out the campaign were Instagram and TikTok. With these platforms being in the top 5 biggest platforms in the world (Biggest Social Media Platforms 2024 | Statista, 2024), Instagram’s wise range of tools that make content pop out and TikTok’s unique algorithm, MOSHPIT’s marketing campaign found its’ home. We chose that based on our target audience, research, and a survey that we held.

    First, our target audience is young people between the age of 18 and 25 years old. They are part of Gen Z and have passion for (hard) rock. Gender and ethnicity do not matter; however, they live in the Netherlands (regardless Dutch or international). Research made showed that the top 3 social media platforms used by Gen Z are YouTube, Instagram and TikTok (Thomas, 2024). Over 80% of them discover new products on social media and they use on average 5 medial platforms daily (Gitnux, 2023). They mostly use social media for 4 hours a day (Shriber, 2024).

    Second, we carried out a survey so we can get a deeper insight into our target group and their social media behavior. The key insight from it were:

  • Most used social media platforms are Instagram and TikTok (91% and 73% votes).
  • Peaks of activity on these platforms are in the evenings (between 6 and 10 pm) and in the nights (between 10 pm and 4 am). 
  • Main purpose of the social medias is entertainment and social activities.  
  • Most consumed content are videos, pictures, and stories (Instagram).  

    • These are the main insights that helped us make definite decisions about the social media platforms we will use and the basis on which the communication marketing plan was made. All the results from the survey can be found in the Appendix Marketing.
    With all this in mind, the platforms chosen had all its required to help us carry out a successful marketing campaign.

However, in the end the response objective was not met. The Instagram account only generated 65 (out of 100) followers and the TikTok only 12 out of the desired 50.

As for the affect objective, we do not consider it a failed one since a community takes time to build but from the first 4 weeks of the campaign the base for such a community is set.

    Last but not least, the response objective regarding the clicks generated on the website was reached successfully. The promotions for it were done on both social media accounts so it can get as much exposure as possible. Even on the first day we already had 6 clicks but by the end they turned into 64 (desired 50).

    Takebacks

    The things learned from this campaign are a lot. From small things like the fact that a content creator shouldn’t forget to make the TikTok profile a business one so they can have statistics to how a campaign is actually supposed to work. During the campaign we learned that Gen Z is a very vulnerable target audience and social media consumers. They have a very short attention span, and it is harder than it looks to create content that they will want to interact with. Some posts were made without a lot of thought to be put and just to keep the aesthetics of the feed, when later on we realized that just posting a solid color as a post can have several meanings that can lead customers to confusion. Moreover, the trends are changing non-stop, and a content creator should really be fully dedicated to following them at all times. Furthermore, very deep and good research on how the algorithms of the platforms is required for the most output. We didn’t expect that it would be that hard to generate followers, especially on TikTok. For such a big platform 50 followers seemed so little and still we managed to get only 10% of them. Also, we expected that the reels on Instagram will do better than they did in terms of attracting users to the main page.A decision that the team made was to split some of the posts in Instagram into multiple once. While we did that so we can create a stronger visual impact (Andrew, 2019), we realized that it might have had a negative influence on the statistics since people also see it as something confusing and just ignore it (Basler, 2023). What did make an impression was that with time the hashtags got better at reaching the right audience and they really did contribute to push our content to as many accounts out there as possible. The content calendar didn’t change much since we based all the decisions for it on the results from surveys and research mentioned above and the team stuck close to it.

    Overall, the team expected to have better results. However, a general learning point is that we might have underestimated the target audience since we are also part of it, and we take that if we like something they will also like it. Things that we would do differently in the future is to post more on both social medias, make more content like pools and voting to actually engage with the target audience and prepare the visuals better.

    Future Planning

    If the project was to continue some changes should be applied. Target audience doesn’t require any changes, however more research should be done in terms of their online behavior and more ways to engage with them should be looked into. Moreover, the communication and media calendar needs to be enriched. In the first 4 weeks an average of 10 posts per week was made. Out of this maximum 3 were on TikTok, which can be seen as something to be improved. The social media themselves recommend 3 posts a day (Macready, 2023), which the team couldn’t do but certainly can improve for the future. On Instagram on the other hand content containing giveaways and prizes (concert tickets or free drinks) should be made. This will increase the interest in the product and make people want to interact (Carter, 2024). Also, as mentioned above a recommendation is to not split the posts on Instagram so often unless it’s necessary. Furthermore, to be able to see how these changes are going to affect the campaigns, new objectives for the brand should be set. Based on past experience and taking into consideration the changes that will be implemented to the social media tactics we recommend objectives as follows:

    Lean Canvas

    Problem

    • Although rock fans are in the community, it's a struggle for them to find company for a concert or an event.

      Social Connection Difficulty: After conducting in-depth problem interviews with rock fans, we discovered a common problem in the community: even if other rock fans are all around them, many people find it difficult to find friends for events or performances. Because of their isolation, they are unable to give their passion their all and take full advantage of the lively rock environment.

    • It's a struggle for rock fans to find events easily and in a convenient way.

      Event Discovery Challenge: Finding relevant events quickly and easily is a major obstacle that rock enthusiasts must overcome, according to our research. Due to the spread of event information over multiple platforms and sources, fans frequently lose out on opportunities to engage with the rock community, which leads to low engagement and a disconnected feeling.

    • Rock fans are often victims of stereotyping because of their appearence.

      Stereotyping and prejudice: In addition, rock enthusiasts voiced their worries about encountering discrimination and stereotypes because of their looks and musical tastes. In addition to hurting their confidence, this unfavourable opinion builds social barriers that make it challenging for them to build meaningful relationships and feel accepted in the community.

    Solution

    • Inform about rock events nearby organized and hosted by us.

      Comprehensive Event Information: We created a solution to solve the difficulty of event discovery by informing rock enthusiasts about local events that we organise and host, drawing on insights from our problem interviews. Our goal is to enable fans to fully immerse themselves in the dynamic rock scene by giving them quick access to comprehensive event information through the selection and organisation of event listings.

    • A community chat and an event chat where rock fans can communicate with each other.

      Community Engagement tools: We integrated community chat and event chat tools into our platform since we understand how important social connections are for rock fans. These interactive tools solve the social connection difficulties found during problem interviews by allowing users to connect with others who share their interests, exchange stories, and create lasting relationships within the rock community.

    • The option to add friends which allows them to find company for an event or a concert.

      Facilitated Companionship: We added the ability to add friends to our platform in response to the difficulty people have in finding companions for events and concerts. By facilitating communication between users and allowing them to plan events, this feature helps users get past social challenges that frequently prevent them from participating in the rock scene.

    Validation of Assumptions

    Before we conducted the problem interviews, we drew a lot of our assumptions from internet forums and discussions where fans of hard rock and rock voiced passionate views. The importance of community and music within the rock scene was highlighted by these online communities, which offered insightful information on the tastes and priorities of rock fans. We developed initial assumptions based on these results, emphasising the value of community involvement, social interaction, and event discovery for rock fans. By speaking with people in the rock world, we were able to confirm and improve these assumptions as we moved through the problem interviews. We were able to check if our initial assumptions about the unique issues and pain points faced by rock fans were in line with their needs and wants through in-depth interviews and feedback sessions. These insights helped us discover that while the community has some of the problems we assumed (Struggling to find events, Hard to connect with other hard rock enthusiasts), it also helped us realise some of the solutions we assumed could help them, like a database of bands, albums and songs for discovery, would not solve the problems rock fans are actually facing. This led us to come up with better possible solutions, which then were validated through the solution interviews, which were an important source of confirmation for the assumptions we made in the last phase of the process. Our assumptions were on the value of a social aspect that would allow fans to interact, exchange stories, and talk about their favourite bands in addition to informing the community about nearby rock events. We were able to verify that these solutions struck a deep chord with our target audience through the solution interviews. These verified assumptions then moved naturally into our Lean Canvas's final version. One of the biggest pivots we have faced based on all the insights we got throughout the different phases of the project was the decision that rather than just informing the rock community about nearby events, it would be more beneficial if we created our own events as it would create stronger connections within the community, while it would also improve our profits. Our solution grew to include features like informing users to local rock events that we host and organise, connecting rock lovers through a community and event chat, and allowing friends to be added to the system to create a buddy system for events and concerts. As confirmed by direct input from our target audience, this connection between our initial assumptions, validated solutions, and final product version shows our dedication to satisfying the real requirements and wishes of rock fans.
